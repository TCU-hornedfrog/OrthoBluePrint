Patella Fracture

Fractures of the patella ‚Äî the largest sesamoid in the body ‚Äî that may or may not disrupt the extensor mechanism of the knee. The critical question every time: is the extensor mechanism intact?

Key concept: The extensor mechanism is the deciding factor ‚Äî not displacement alone. A patient who can perform an active straight leg raise may be managed nonoperatively regardless of fracture gap.
01

Epidemiology & Mechanism

Direct Mechanism
Fall / Dashboard Blow
Direct blow to the anterior knee. Results in comminuted or stellate patterns. Overlying skin is often compromised ‚Äî always inspect carefully for open fracture.
Indirect Mechanism
Eccentric Quadriceps Contraction
Sudden deceleration or stumble with eccentric quad contraction. Results in a transverse fracture pattern at mid-patella. Extensor mechanism typically disrupted.
02

Anatomy & Extensor Mechanism

The patella is a sesamoid within the quadriceps tendon. It improves the mechanical advantage of the quadriceps by 30‚Äì50% by increasing the lever arm at the knee. The complete extensor mechanism: quadriceps ‚Üí patella ‚Üí patellar tendon ‚Üí tibial tubercle. Disruption anywhere in this chain eliminates active extension.

The medial and lateral retinaculum can maintain extensor mechanism continuity even with a displaced transverse fracture. This is why the straight leg raise test ‚Äî not X-ray displacement ‚Äî determines operative indication.

04

Presentation

Anterior knee pain, swelling, and bruising after mechanism of injury. Palpable defect at the fracture site in displaced fractures. The critical exam finding: can the patient actively extend the knee against gravity? Inability to do so ‚Äî the extensor mechanism is disrupted and surgery is required.

05

Imaging

X-rays: AP + lateral + Merchant (axial/sunrise) view. The lateral view best shows transverse fractures and patellar height. The Merchant view evaluates vertical fractures and patellofemoral alignment.

Bipartite patella: Superolateral location, smooth corticated edges, bilateral in ~50%. Do NOT mistake for fracture. Compare contralateral knee ‚Äî the location is consistent in bipartite patella.
06

Treatment

01
Nonoperative
Intact extensor mechanism, <3mm step, <2mm gap
‚ñº
Criteria (all must be met)
  • Patient can perform active straight leg raise
  • <3 mm articular step-off
  • <2 mm fracture gap
Management
  • Cylinder cast or hinged knee brace in extension √ó 4‚Äì6 weeks
  • Progressive weight bearing and ROM after immobilization
The intact extensor mechanism is the deciding factor ‚Äî not radiographic displacement alone. Always test the SLR before deciding on management.
02
Tension Band Wiring ‚Äî ORIF
Displaced transverse fractures with disrupted extensor mechanism
‚ñº
Biomechanical Principle
  • Converts tensile forces on the anterior patella into compressive forces at the articular surface during knee flexion
  • Longitudinal K-wires or cannulated screws + figure-of-8 wire anteriorly
Technical Key
  • Figure-of-8 wire must be placed anterior to the mid-axis of the patella to function as a true tension band
  • Wire placed posteriorly fails to compress the articular surface
Wire position is everything. Anterior to mid-axis = compression at joint. Posterior to mid-axis = distraction at joint. This is a classic pimp point.
03
Partial / Total Patellectomy
Severely comminuted, unreconstructable fractures
‚ñº
Indications
  • Severely comminuted fracture not amenable to internal fixation
  • Poor bone quality ‚Äî hardware cannot be held
  • Failed prior fixation
Tradeoffs
  • Partial preferred ‚Äî preserves lever arm and quadriceps strength
  • Total patellectomy reduces quadriceps strength by 30‚Äì50%
Preserve as much patella as possible ‚Äî even a partial patella maintains significantly more quadriceps mechanical advantage than total patellectomy.
07

Complications

Hardware Irritation Most Common
K-wire migration and prominent hardware are the most common complication of tension band wiring. Often requires hardware removal at 12 months.
Loss of Reduction
Wire placed posterior to mid-axis fails to compress the articular surface. Construct fails under early ROM loading.
Extensor Lag
Inability to fully extend the knee. Related to inadequate repair, insufficient rehabilitation, or quadriceps weakness.
Patellofemoral Arthritis
Articular malreduction or comminution leads to post-traumatic arthritis at the patellofemoral joint.
AVN of Patella Fragment
Rare. Blood supply enters distally and proximally. Displaced fractures can devascularize a fragment.
Wound Dehiscence
Thin skin directly over the patella is at risk, especially with direct mechanism injuries with skin contusion.

Pocket Pimped

‚ùì Attending-Level Questions
What is the single most important factor in deciding operative vs. nonoperative management? Tap to reveal
The integrity of the extensor mechanism ‚Äî not radiographic displacement. If the patient can actively extend the knee against gravity (positive straight leg raise), the extensor mechanism is intact and nonoperative management is appropriate provided the articular step is <3 mm and gap <2 mm. A displaced fracture with intact retinaculum can still be managed nonoperatively.
Explain the biomechanical principle of tension band wiring and why wire position matters. Tap to reveal
The anterior surface of the patella is under tension during knee flexion due to quadriceps pull. Placing a figure-of-8 wire on the anterior surface converts this tension into compression at the articular surface ‚Äî promoting fracture healing. The wire must be anterior to the mid-axis of the patella. If placed posteriorly (closer to the articular surface), it acts as a fulcrum and distracts the articular surface rather than compressing it.
What is a bipartite patella and how do you differentiate it from a fracture? Tap to reveal
A bipartite patella is a developmental variant where the superolateral pole fails to fuse ‚Äî present in ~2‚Äì3% of the population. Key distinguishing features: superolateral location (consistent), smooth corticated edges (no sharp fracture line), bilateral in approximately 50% of cases, and the patient often has no history of acute trauma. Fractures have sharp, irregular edges and correlate with the mechanism and point of tenderness.
How much does total patellectomy reduce quadriceps strength and why? Tap to reveal
Total patellectomy reduces quadriceps strength by approximately 30‚Äì50%. The patella functions as a sesamoid that anteriorly displaces the patellar tendon from the knee's center of rotation, increasing the lever arm of the quadriceps. Without the patella, the tendon lies closer to the joint's axis of rotation and generates less torque per unit of muscle force ‚Äî particularly affecting terminal extension.
What is a sleeve fracture of the patella and why is it commonly missed? Tap to reveal
A pediatric injury where the cartilaginous sleeve of the distal patellar pole is avulsed, carrying only a tiny osseous fragment. On X-ray, the bony fragment looks small and inconsequential ‚Äî but it carries a large cartilaginous component. The key finding is patella alta (proximal retraction of the patella) with a small distal bony fragment. Missed diagnoses result in chronic extensor mechanism insufficiency.
What is the most common complication requiring reoperation after tension band wiring? Tap to reveal
Hardware irritation ‚Äî K-wire migration and prominence of the figure-of-8 wire beneath the thin anterior patellar skin. The wires can migrate proximally or distally and cause pain with kneeling, clothing friction, or scar irritation. Hardware removal at approximately 12 months after confirmed healing is commonly performed and provides reliable symptom relief.
